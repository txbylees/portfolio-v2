The Challenge

A healthcare portal where errors aren't just bugs — they're patient safety risks

Nexus Health was launching a patient management portal used by clinical staff across multiple departments. The stakes were unusually high: incorrect data display, broken workflows, or compliance failures could directly impact patient care. The system also needed to satisfy data privacy regulations before going live.

The development team were strong technically but lacked experience testing in a regulated healthcare environment. I was brought in to own the QA process, design the test strategy, and coordinate UAT with clinical stakeholders who had never participated in formal testing before.

The Approach

Clinical-first test design with real stakeholder involvement

The key insight was that no test suite could be credible without clinical input. I structured the process to bring actual end-users — nurses, administrators, and clinicians — into the testing cycle in a structured, guided way.

01

Requirements Analysis

Reviewed clinical workflows with department leads to understand real-world usage patterns. Mapped these directly to testable acceptance criteria, surfacing ambiguities in requirements before any testing began.

02

Compliance Testing

Validated data handling against applicable privacy standards — covering data access controls, audit logging, session timeouts, and data minimisation across all portal features.

03

Functional & Regression Testing

Executed 60+ test cases across the full clinical workflow suite — patient registration, appointment scheduling, record access, and discharge processes. Ran full regression after each sprint release.

04

Facilitated UAT Sessions

Ran structured UAT sessions with clinical staff across 8 role types. Documented feedback, translated issues into actionable bug reports, and guided the team through three rounds of UAT until full sign-off was achieved.

The Result

100% UAT sign-off, zero compliance findings at launch

The portal launched on schedule with full UAT sign-off from all clinical departments and no compliance findings raised during the subsequent regulatory review. Defects caught during testing — including two data access control issues that would have been compliance breaches — were resolved before any patient data was processed.

The UAT process we built became the standard template used for future Nexus Health product launches.
